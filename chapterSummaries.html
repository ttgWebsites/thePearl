Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter #1
  • The novella begins at sunrise as Kino awakes.
  • He and his wife and son live a straw house on the Mexican coast, where he works as a pearl diver.
  • As the day begins Juana lights a fire and prepares a humble breakfast.
  • Kino is content with the simplicity of his life. As they eat their breakfast the couple notices a scorpion crawling near their infant son, Coyotito.
  • The scorpion bites the baby before the parents can stop it.
  • Because the local doctor is discriminatory toward the poor, Kino doubts that he would come to the family’s lower-class home to heal the bite.
  • Kino and Juana set out to the town to beg the upper-class doctor for his assistance with the accompaniment of neighbors and onlookers.
  • Once at the doctor’s house, Kino and Juana implore the doctor for his help.
  • The doctor rejects Kino’s meager payment and tells his servant to tell the couple that he is not home.
  • Angry and ashamed Kino returns home with Juana and ailing Coyotito.
  • Chapter #2
  • Kino and Juana set out in Kino’s canoe in hopes of finding a valuable enough pearl that the doctor will accept as payment.
  • The canoe is Kino’s sole possession of any value.
  • Juana and Coyotito wait in the canoe as Kino searches the sea for pearls and Juana treats Coyotito’s wound with seaweed.
  • Kino searches the oyster bed for the largest shells. He finds a particularly huge oyster and brings it back into the canoe.
  • Kino opens the oyster to discover the most enormous pearl that he had ever seen.
  • Just as they discover the pearl, Coyotito’s wound miraculously heals.
  • Kino lets out a triumphant cry in face of such an extraordinary turn of events.
  • The other divers around them hear Kino’s yell and gather to determine the motivation for his exclamation.
  • Chapter #3
  • By the time the family returned to their home, the whole town had heard of Kino’s discovery.
  • Each person who heard dreamt of the things they would do with the wealth from the giant pearl.
  • Kino and Juana feel incredibly fortunate to have found their great treasure, they too dream of the things they will do with the wealth.
  • Along with all of Kino’s friends and neighbors, the local priest visits the family.
  • Even he desires the fortune of the pearl. As the family prepares to go to sleep, the doctor and his servant arrive at the house.
  • Even though Coyotito appears to be healed, Juana and Kino allow the doctor to examine their son.
  • The doctor poisons and then “heals” the baby so that he may collect a larger payment.
  • That night the family wakes in the middle of the night to the sounds of someone searching their hut. Kino and the intruder fight and the trespasser flees, after Kino injures him with his knife.
  • Juana begins to believe that the pearl may be more of a curse than a blessing.
  • They awake to a morning very different from the day before, Kino hopeful and Juana apprehensive about their pearl.
  • Chapter #4
  • Kino, Juana, and Coyotito, prepared to sell their magnificent pearl, set out for the village along with Kino’s brother, their neighbors, and the local pearl-divers.
  • Kino and his brother Juan Thomas approach the first pearl buyer, he tells them that the pearl is too large and makes Kino a very low offer.
  • Other local pearl buyers appraise the pearl and offer Kino similarly low prices for his treasure.
  • Kino rejects them and decides to go to the capital to sell the pearl.
  • That night Kino does not sleep; he is too concerned with protecting the pearl that will guarantee a good life for his son.
  • Once again someone attempts to steal the pearl, this time injuring Kino with a knife.
  • Despite his injuries Kino is determined to canoe to the capital the next morning.
  • Juana is even more certain of the pearl’s evil. She begs her husband to discard it, Kino refuses and Juana submits to Kino’s plan.
  • Chapter #5
  • Still night, Kino awakes to find Juana taking the pearl to the beach.
  • Kino follows her and assaults her to prevent her from throwing the pearl in the ocean.
  • Kino leaves his unconscious wife on the beach, only to be attacked by a group of men on his way home, Kino stabs and kills on of his assailants.
  • Juana awakes to find the pearl that had been knocked from Kino’s grasp.
  • She aids her husband and tells him that they must run before people discover that Kino has murdered a man.
  • Kino’s canoe was destroyed and his house was set on fire. Kino, Juana, and Coyotito hide for the at his brother’s house.
  • The next night, the three set out for the capital where they can sell their pearl.
  • Chapter #6
  • Kino, Juana, and Coyotito walk through the forest all night.
  • They stop and make camp in the morning. Kino awakes hearing a group of men tracking them.
  • The family flees through the mountains. Kino wants to fight his pursuers, but Juana refuses to go on without him.
  • Despite their attempts to conceal the trail, the trackers still catch up to the family. Kino, Juana, and Coyotito hide in a cave for the night while the trackers camp nearby.
  • Feeling trapped, Kino plans to kill one of the men so that he and his family can escape during the night.
  • Before Kino can kill the watchman, Coyotito begins to cry, waking the trackers. One of the men fires his gun in the direction of the wail.
  • Kino attacks the three men. He stabs one, knocks another unconscious, and shoots the last man.
  • The next thing Kino hears is a scream from his wife, the bullet that the tracker fired had killed Coyotito.
  • The next day Kino and Juana return to their village carrying the body of their son. Onlookers stare in silence as the couple walks to the sea.
  • Kino takes the evil pearl from his pocket and casts it into the ocean.
